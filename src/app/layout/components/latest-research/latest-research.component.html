Stereotactic Radiosurgery (SRS)
SRS is a non-invasive treatment technique that delivers highly focused radiation beams to the tumor with pinpoint accuracy. It is commonly used for small or inaccessible brain tumors and helps to spare healthy brain tissue.

Research on brain tumors

Research on brain tumors is an extensive field that aims to understand the causes, development, treatment, and prevention of various types of brain tumors. Scientists and medical professionals conduct research to improve diagnostic methods, develop more effective treatments, and improve the overall care provided to patients with brain tumors. Here are some key areas of research in the field of brain tumors:

  • Genetic Studies

    Researchers study the genetic factors that contribute to the development of brain tumors. This involves identifying specific genes and mutations associated with different types of brain tumors. Genetic research can help understand the underlying mechanisms of tumor formation and guide personalized treatment approaches.

  • Biomarkers and Diagnostic Tools

    Scientists are exploring the use of biomarkers, such as proteins or genetic markers, that can indicate the presence of brain tumors. Developing accurate and non-invasive diagnostic tools is crucial for early detection and timely treatment.

  • Treatment Strategies

    Researchers investigate various treatment modalities for brain tumors, including surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, targeted therapies, immunotherapy, and combination therapies. The goal is to develop more precise and effective treatment strategies that improve patient outcomes while minimizing side effects.

  • Clinical Trials

    Clinical trials play a critical role in evaluating new therapies and treatment approaches for brain tumors. Researchers conduct trials to assess the safety and efficacy of novel drugs, treatments, and treatment combinations. Participation in clinical trials allows patients to access potentially groundbreaking treatments and contribute to advancements in the field.

  • Imaging Techniques

    Advances in imaging technologies, such as MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging), PET (Positron Emission Tomography), and advanced radiological techniques, aid in the diagnosis, characterization, and monitoring of brain tumors. Researchers continue to improve imaging methods to enhance tumor visualization, track treatment response, and guide surgical interventions.

  • Tumor Microenvironment

    The tumor microenvironment refers to the surrounding cells, blood vessels, and immune system components that interact with tumor cells. Research focuses on understanding the complex interactions within the tumor microenvironment to identify vulnerabilities and develop targeted therapies that disrupt tumor growth and promote an anti-tumor immune response.

  • Brain Tumor Classification

    Efforts are underway to refine the classification and grading systems for brain tumors. Research aims to establish more precise and clinically relevant criteria for tumor classification, which can aid in treatment decision-making and prognosis prediction.

  • Supportive Care and Quality of Life

    Research also addresses the psychosocial aspects of brain tumor management. Studies explore interventions to improve the quality of life for patients and their families, including supportive care, psychological support, rehabilitation programs, and survivorship care.

It is important to note that research in the field of brain tumors is an ongoing and dynamic process. New discoveries and advancements continue to emerge, shaping the understanding and treatment of these complex conditions. Collaborative efforts between researchers, healthcare professionals, and patients play a crucial role in driving progress and improving outcomes for individuals affected by brain tumors.
